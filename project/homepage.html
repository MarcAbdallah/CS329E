What is Climate Change?

According to Oxford Languages, climate change is defined as "a change in global or regional climate patterns, in particular a change apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards and attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels."

NASA states that the global phenomena known as climate change are due to the burning of fossil fuels which add "heat-trapping gases to Earth's atmosphere." These phenomena include increased temperatures, the rising of sea levels, ice mass loss, changes in the blooming of plant life, and extreme weather events.

What is the Green Team?

The Green Team is a group of students from the University of Texas at Austin taking CS329E, Elements of Web Programming.

Our goal is to educate others about climate change and raise awareness about its causes and effects. A climate crisis is already happening, and the faster we take action the better.

The Future of Climate Change

Human Life and Prosperity
Human health will deteriorate because rising temperatures have led to a rise in smog which can cause lung disease, heart disease, and asthma. Agriculture is severely threatened as farmers are consistently struggling to defend their crops from the rapidly changing weather patterns which can cause an increase in diseases, pests and weeds. Crops are also affected by the rise in extreme weather. For example, flooding or drought.
Ocean Life and Extreme Weather
Ocean life will continue to be affected because approximately a third of carbon dioxide emissions end up in the oceans which causes the water to become more acidic and, in turn, destroys the shells of sea life. Also, because of the rising temperatures, levels of evaporation are increasing which lead to more severe and common hurricanes.
Natural Habitats
A number of natural habitats will continue to be harmed. The ice that Arctic animals survive on is disappearing, coral and shellfish are dying due to rising sea temperatures, and forests are becoming more prone to infestations because the changing atmosphere allows deadly insects to survive longer.
